# Personas

Three primary personas drive Align's priorities. Each maps to a core tab and a core
emotional job.

## Primary — Sarah, 29 · Aspiring homeowner

- **Context:** Disciplined saver, single income, building a down-payment fund.
- **Goal:** Know whether her plan is "enough" and reaches the date she's targeting.
- **Frustration:** Spreadsheets and account apps tell her balances, not trajectory.
  She over-checks and still feels anxious.
- **What success feels like:** Reassurance grounded in math — "On Track, projected
  Aug 2027, here's why" — not another rule to follow.
- **Maps to:** Goals tab, Goal Alignment Score, milestone projection.

## Primary — Marcus, 38 · Values-conscious spender

- **Context:** Comfortable income, but money "leaks" through small recurring choices.
- **Goal:** Spend with intention, aligned to what he cares about — without guilt.
- **Frustration:** Budgeting apps scold him with red overspent bars; he tunes them out.
- **What success feels like:** A factual, non-judgmental nudge — "Dining is trending
  24% above your goal-aligned pace; at this rate your down payment shifts ~6 weeks" —
  that he can act on or knowingly accept.
- **Maps to:** Money tab, alignment indicators, Predictive Guidance.

## Primary — Jordan, 47 · Hands-off investor

- **Context:** Portfolio spread across 6+ accounts (401k, IRA, Roth, taxable, ESPP, 529).
- **Goal:** Stay diversified and on-track for retirement/education without becoming an
  expert or hiring an advisor.
- **Frustration:** No consolidated view; doesn't know if allocation has drifted.
- **What success feels like:** Accounts mapped to goals, drift flagged against an
  *educational reference* allocation, clearly labeled as not personalized advice.
- **Maps to:** Invest tab, allocation drift, goal mapping.

## Anti-persona

The user seeking active trading, hot stock tips, or gamified streaks. Align
deliberately does not serve them — see the compassion and information-not-advice
principles in [`../DESIGN.md`](../DESIGN.md).
