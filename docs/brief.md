# Brief — Align

## Problem

Most personal-finance tools are **retrospective** (what did you spend?) and
**account-centric** (here are your balances). A user with 3–7 accounts has no
coherent narrative across them and no way to answer the only question that matters:
*what does today's spending mean for the life I'm trying to build?* The result is
either anxiety (am I doing enough?) or avoidance (don't look).

## Product in one line

Align is a goals-driven personal finance app that renders every financial screen
through the lens of user-defined life goals — not accounts.

## Why now

Account aggregation (Plaid) is commoditized; the differentiator is no longer *seeing*
your money but *interpreting* it. LLM synthesis plus deterministic projection makes
plain-language, source-traceable guidance feasible without crossing into advice.

## Target users

Three primary personas — disciplined-but-anxious saver, intentional-but-leaky
spender, hands-off multi-account investor. Detail in [`personas.md`](./personas.md).

## Success looks like

A user can answer "am I on track, and why?" in one glance, and returns weekly without
dread. Measurable definitions in [`success-metrics.md`](./success-metrics.md).

## Out of scope (v1 / MVP)

- Trade execution or money movement (investing is **read-only**).
- Personalized investment advice or specific securities recommendations (regulatory).
- Auto-canceling subscriptions (we surface, the user decides).
- Streaks, leaderboards, or any shame/deficit mechanic.
- Native dark mode (proposed, not shipped — see [`open-questions.md`](./open-questions.md)).
